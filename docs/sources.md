## 6. Interiority — Literary & Architectural Foundations

**Literary interiority:** A character's thoughts, feelings, and inner struggles — accessed through first or third person. A character with plot but no interiority is a puppet. (Castiglia 2015, Kidlit 2017, Maum 2023)

**Architectural interiority:** "Inhabitation" — the traces left behind by a dweller. An empty room is architecture. A room with traces of its occupant is interiority. (Atmodiwirjo & Yatmo 2023; Walter Benjamin, 1936)

| Source | Type | Verified |
|--------|------|----------|
| Hester L. Castiglia — interiority in literature (2015) | Academic | ✅ |
| Kidlit — craft writing on interiority (2017) | Craft reference | ✅ |
| Rebecca Maum — interiority in writing (2023) | Craft reference | ✅ |
| Atmodiwirjo & Yatmo — architectural interiority (2023) | Academic | ✅ |
| Walter Benjamin — "To dwell means to leave traces" (1936) | Philosophical | ✅ |

**Relevance to dot-self:** AIS synthesizes both definitions. Literary interiority = `agent-self.md` + `diary/` (the inner life). Architectural interiority = `journal/` + `sanctuary/` + the room itself (the traces of inhabitation).
